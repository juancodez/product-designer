Flora
App

Client Flora Labs
Year 2024
Role Product Designer
Duration 4 months

Flora is a plant care companion for people who love plants but struggle to keep them alive. We designed an AI-powered app that identifies plants by photo, diagnoses problems, and builds personalised care schedules. FLORA
The Challenge

A market full of
plant apps, none
built for humans.

See our approach

The plant care app market was crowded — but almost entirely built for people who already know what they're doing. Apps skewed toward botanical databases and latin species names. They assumed knowledge the average user didn't have, and they made caring for a plant feel like a homework assignment.

Flora Labs had a clear vision: a plant app for the person who keeps buying succulents and watching them die. But the early prototype read like a gardening encyclopedia. Onboarding asked users to identify their plant species before they could do anything — a wall that killed 74% of sessions on the first screen.

The engineering team had already built impressive AI infrastructure for plant recognition. The problem was entirely in the experience layer: how to make powerful AI feel effortless, and how to build a daily care habit that actually sticks.

Our Approach

Conversational,
caring, and
actually joyful.

We rebuilt onboarding around a single moment: point your phone at a plant, and Flora tells you everything. No forms, no species selection, no Latin names. The AI does the heavy lifting; the user gets an instant name, a health check, and a care schedule — in under 30 seconds.

The core interaction model shifted from a reference tool to a care companion. Flora messages you when a plant needs water, warns you about seasonal changes, and celebrates when your plant is thriving. The notification system was designed as a character, not a task manager — using tone, timing, and gentle humour to build a daily habit.

Every micro-interaction was prototyped and tested with a panel of 40 plant owners at different experience levels. We ran A/B tests on six different onboarding flows before landing on the camera-first approach. Gamification elements — streaks, collection milestones, and a "plant health score" — were validated against retention data in a two-week closed beta.

The Results

What we
delivered.

Flora launched on iOS and Android simultaneously in March 2024. The App Store feature drove 170,000 installs in the first 48 hours. Day-30 retention — the number that had kept Flora Labs up at night — came in at 41%, well above the category average of 24%.

The care notification system, designed as a character rather than an alert, achieved a 62% open rate — a number the team initially assumed was a data error. Users were opening the app not because a task demanded it, but because Flora had become a small presence in their day they actually looked forward to.

Flora Labs closed their Seed round at $2.4M in April 2024, citing the product's retention metrics as the primary signal for investor confidence.

What wasn't working

  • ✕ Onboarding required species identification before any interaction — 74% drop-off
  • ✕ Interface felt like a botanical reference tool, not a care app
  • ✕ Notifications were generic reminders — no personality, low open rate
  • ✕ No retention mechanics — users had no reason to return after initial setup
  • ✕ Day-7 retention below 14%, well under the category benchmark

What we improved

  • ✓ Camera-first onboarding — plant identified, schedule built in under 30 seconds
  • ✓ Conversational care companion tone across all touchpoints
  • ✓ Character-driven notifications with 62% open rate
  • ✓ Streaks, milestones, and health scoring driving daily habit formation
  • ✓ Day-30 retention at 41% — 70% above the category average
