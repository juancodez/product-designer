Client Nexus Analytics GmbH

Nexus Analytics provides data intelligence for mid-market enterprises. NEXUS
The Challenge

Where five years
of growth became
a usability debt.

See our approach

The Nexus platform had been built incrementally by a rotating engineering team with no dedicated design ownership. Every new feature was bolted onto the existing interface — a third navigation tier here, a new widget type there. Five years in, the cognitive load was crippling user adoption.

Power users had developed workarounds: bookmarks to specific drill-down URLs, custom browser scripts to hide panels they never used. The onboarding funnel showed that 68% of new users never reached the core analytics view in their first session.

The engineering team had the data to understand what was wrong. What they lacked was a framework to fix it without disrupting the power users who relied on the existing structure — however broken it was.

Our Approach

Data-first design,
built around
real workflows.

Before touching Figma, we spent three weeks in discovery — 22 user interviews, session recordings analysis, and a full task-flow audit. We mapped every distinct job-to-be-done across the user base: from the data engineer building pipelines to the C-suite executive reading weekly KPI summaries.

The core insight: most users needed only 20% of the platform's features to complete 80% of their work. The interface was hiding that 20% inside the noise of everything else. The new information architecture built adaptive surfaces — showing complexity only when the workflow demanded it.

We ran three two-week design sprints with the engineering team embedded throughout, using a shared Figma component library as the single source of truth. Every component was built for both light and dark mode, accessibility-first, and mobile-responsive from day one.

The Results

What we
delivered.

The redesigned Nexus platform launched to beta users in Q3 2024. Within eight weeks, the onboarding completion rate climbed from 32% to 79%. The engineering team reported a 60% reduction in UI-related support tickets in the first month post-launch.

The design system became the foundation for the company's entire product suite — reducing the time to ship new features by an estimated 35% by eliminating design-from-scratch work for every new component.

Nexus used the redesigned product as a core part of their Series A pitch deck. The round closed at €4.2M in December 2024.

What wasn't working

  • ✕ Three nested navigation levels requiring 6–8 clicks to reach core views
  • ✕ 60+ widget types with no grouping logic or contextual relevance
  • ✕ No mobile support — critical for executive users reviewing dashboards on the go
  • ✕ No design system: every new feature diverged from the last
  • ✕ 68% of new users never completed onboarding in their first session

What we improved

  • ✓ Single contextual navigation layer — core views reachable in 1–2 clicks
  • ✓ Progressive disclosure: complexity revealed only when the workflow needs it
  • ✓ Full mobile parity — responsive across all breakpoints
  • ✓ 80+ component design system shared between design and engineering
  • ✓ Onboarding completion rate up from 32% to 79% in 8 weeks
